Disenrollment Rate by State

PMPM: Disenrolled vs. Retained Members

Finding: Annual disenrollment stands at 11.6%, against a target of under 8%. Premium affordability (28.0%) and network adequacy / access issues (43.0% combined) drive the majority of exits. Disenrolled members run higher average PMPM ($1,769.90) than retained members ($1,302.13), meaning the plan is losing members who are already costlier to serve — access and affordability fixes here double as a retention and cost play.

Provider Quality vs. Cost — Risk Adjustment
Testing whether higher PCP quality scores correlate with higher cost, before and after adjusting for member risk score

Raw Member PMPM by Attributed PCP Quality Score

Risk-Adjusted PMPM by Attributed PCP Quality Score

Finding: Raw PMPM does not increase cleanly with PCP quality score — the relationship is irregular and driven mainly by which risk pool each PCP happens to carry. Once PMPM is divided by each member's risk score, the pattern flattens further, confirming that a single blended PMPM number is not a reliable stand-alone measure of provider performance without controlling for line of business and case mix.
